Arthur Edelstein is a software engineer focused on human wellbeing. At Tor, he introduced stream isolation, where Tor Browser uses [a separate circuit for each website you visit](https://support.torproject.org/tor-browser/features/managing-identities/#:~:text=The%20URL%20bar). At Firefox, he was the product manager for the teams that developed [Total Cookie Protection](https://blog.mozilla.org/security/2021/02/23/total-cookie-protection/) and [HTTPS-Only Mode](https://blog.mozilla.org/security/2020/11/17/firefox-83-introduces-https-only-mode/). At Brave, he introduced [HTTPS by Default](https://brave.com/privacy-updates/22-https-by-default/) and co-authored a paper on the [Pool-Party Attack](https://brave.com/research/files/pool-party-usenix-2023.pdf) on privacy. In 2021, he published [PrivacyTests.org](https://privacytests.org), a project to hold web browser makers accountable for their leaks of private information. He also co-created [Project Timestamper](https://projecttimestamper.org), which is an ongoing effort to preserve the authenticity of human knowledge and culture in the age of AI.
